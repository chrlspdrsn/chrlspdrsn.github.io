Overview: Using Android Studio as a development platform and Java, myself along with two others worked to make a prototype application to help students manage homework, scheduling and classes. We aimed to implement:

    Learnability: simple to learn for first time users
    Memorability: easy to remember after not using app for a long time
    Efficiency: quick and effortless to perform tasks
    Satisfaction: surveys showed positive feedback for the app
    Accessibility: settings allow for changing visuals
Roles: For the project, I was the project manager, created the home and tasks page, and developed our user testing. As project manager, I learned more about leadership through dividing roles and responsibilities among my team for the planning, execution and completion of the project. In addition, I learned a lot about Java, as well as general app development and design. Through the project, I thoroughly enjoyed working in a team setting. It made the project easier to tackle.

Debussy "Ballade" Piano Performance

In addition to my major and two minors, I have been studying Piano under Professor Manami Kawamura since Spring 2019 as an extracurricular. For my second semester, I memorized and performed Debussy's "Ballade". This piece was composed by French impressionist Claude Debussy in 1890, and spans around 7 minutes and 8 pages. It was incredibly challenging and even more rewarding to learn this piece. Below is a video recording from the Student Recital Concert.


Through this preparation and performance, I learned:

    Discipline: In order to successfully learn and perform a piece like this, the pianist must have discipline to train, practice and pay attention to the intricate details and technicalities within the piece.
    Perseverance: From consistent practice up until the end of the performance, I had to have perseverance to not give up when the song got difficult or, while performing, to not stop when I make a mistake.
    Confidence: I had to learn to have confidence in my ability when playing in front of an audience so that I could successfully perform. A pep talk at myself really helped with this one.
